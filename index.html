My trip to Budapest, Hungary

Read time: 5 minutes

Budapest completely stole my heart from the moment I arrived. The way the Danube winds through the city, with the stunning Parliament Building and Buda Castle standing tall on either side, is simply breathtaking. I loved wandering through the charming streets of the Castle District, and there’s nothing quite like relaxing in the famous thermal baths after a day of exploring. The city has this perfect balance of historic beauty and lively, modern culture. The ruin bars are so unique, and I couldn't get enough of the street food. Budapest is definitely a place I'll always want to return to.

'i could live off chimney cakes' - Me, 2024

Day 1: Exploring the Heart of Budapest On my first day, I started with a visit to St. Stephen’s Basilica, marveling at its stunning interior and climbing to the top for panoramic views of the city. From there, I strolled down Andrássy Avenue, stopping at the Hungarian State Opera House. Lunch was at a cozy café, where I enjoyed some traditional goulash. In the afternoon, I crossed the Chain Bridge and headed up to Buda Castle. I spent the rest of the day exploring the Castle District, visiting the Matthias Church and enjoying the views from the Fisherman’s Bastion. I ended the evening with a Danube river cruise, watching the city light up.

Day 2: History and Relaxation Day two started with a visit to Heroes' Square and a walk through Városliget (City Park), where I visited Vajdahunyad Castle. Then, I spent a few relaxing hours at the Széchenyi Thermal Baths, soaking in the warm waters and trying out the various pools. Afterward, I wandered through Váci Street for a bit of shopping before heading to the Central Market Hall to pick up some Hungarian paprika and other souvenirs. The day wrapped up with a visit to one of Budapest's famous ruin bars in the Jewish Quarter, where I soaked in the unique atmosphere.

Day 3: Cultural Immersion I dedicated my last day to immersing myself in Budapest's culture. I started at the House of Terror Museum, learning about Hungary’s more recent history, then took a relaxing afternoon walk along the Danube Promenade, stopping by the Shoes on the Danube Bank memorial. For lunch, I indulged in some lángos, a delicious deep-fried flatbread. Later, I visited the Hungarian Parliament Building—its Gothic Revival architecture is breathtaking up close. I ended the day at Gellért Hill, climbing to the Citadella for incredible sunset views over the city.
